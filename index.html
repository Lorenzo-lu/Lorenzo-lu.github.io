I am pursuing a doctorate in Electrical Engineering at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. My research area is computational imaging/photography. Under the guidance of prof. Andreas Velten, I am now developing a computatinal fluorescence lifetime camera for medical applications.

I was conferred with an M.S. in Materials Science and a B.S. in Geochemistry. My experiences in different majors contributed not only to my knowledge but also to my philosophy, which affects my understanding towards the world and my methodologies to solve the problems.

I have a strong insterest towards the progress and technologies in other areas. In my leisure, I usually learn new skills from online courses or people from different majors. One of my recent interest is defining a coding format to minimize bugs. For instance, for research programs in Python, I prefer a passing-by-reference approach to avoid the casual variable declarations in Python. The function-oriented-programming is widely used in my research code. I even developed tools modifying PyTorch to fit my programming habits.

From my point of view, a good code won't require the coder to pay extra efforts to the work that can be done by computers. Especially, for researchers, the codes should never contain a bunch of variables with abstract names (actually, this can be fixed by building a tree structure), and should be highly modularized so that you won't make a huge adaption when you advisor comes up with some 'novel' ideas.
