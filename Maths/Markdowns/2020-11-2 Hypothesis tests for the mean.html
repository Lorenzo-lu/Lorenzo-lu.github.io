Hypothesis tests for the mean

The problem of deciding which of several possibilities is true is called hypothesis testing. When having two competing claims, we give the benefit of doubt to one (NULL hypothesis) and burden of proof to the other (ALTERNATIVE hypothesis).

Decison rule

Suppose {Xi}\{X_i\}{Xi​} is the set of identical independent random variables from the same distribution. We know when the sample capcity is great, the ensemble mean converges to the expectation of this distribution. But when we don't have that many samples, the ensemble mean doesn't equal the expectation.

Types of error

We can make two types of errors between choosing the null hypothesis and alternative hypothesis.

  1. We declare the alternative hypothesis while the null hypothesis is true. (False positive)
  2. We declare the null hypothesis while the alternative hypothesis is true. (False negative)

The null hypothesis has the benefit of doubt, so we want to bound the probability of rejecting it by mistake. α\alphaα, the significant level, is used to bound the Type I error value.
